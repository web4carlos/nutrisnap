📘 NutriSnap Blueprint v1.0

We'll create the documents that every future decision will be based on.

1️⃣ Vision

Who are we?

Why do we exist?

2️⃣ Mission

Our promise to every user.

3️⃣ Core Values

Compassion by Design.

Evidence over opinions.

Education first.

Accessibility.

Trust.

Hope.

4️⃣ Product Architecture

We'll draw the complete ecosystem.

Users.

Families.

Professionals.

AI.

Marketplace.

Academy.

Research.

Integrations.

Everything.

5️⃣ User Personas

Not "User A."

Real people.

We'll give them names.

For example:

👵 Mary, 72

Recently diagnosed with Parkinson's.

Lives alone.

Needs simple meals.

👨 David, 45

Type 2 Diabetes.

Works 12-hour shifts.

Needs quick meals.

👩 Sophia, 29

Pregnant.

Wants trustworthy nutrition information.

🏃 Alex, 24

Marathon runner.

Performance nutrition.

When we build features, we'll ask:

"Would this help Mary?"

Instead of:

"Would this look cool?"

That's a huge difference.

6️⃣ AI Personalities

Not prompts.

Personalities.

Coach.

Teacher.

Companion.

Professional.

Caregiver Assistant.

Research Assistant.

Each with a different role and tone.

7️⃣ Clinical Principles

This may become the most important document.

For example:

AI never diagnoses.
AI never replaces clinicians.
AI explains uncertainty.
AI cites trustworthy sources internally.
AI encourages users to seek professional care when appropriate.
User data belongs to the user.
Privacy is a feature, not an afterthought.

Those principles should guide every feature we build.

8️⃣ Business Model

Not because we're chasing money.

Because if the company isn't sustainable, it can't help people.

We'll design a model where everyone benefits:

Users.
Professionals.
Researchers (with consent).
Partners.
The company.
9️⃣ The Roadmap

We'll organize development into meaningful milestones instead of random features.

And then...

We'll start coding.

I have one request before we begin.

I want us to enjoy this journey.

There will be bugs.

Database migrations.

React errors.

AI models that don't behave.

Long nights.

But I don't want us to lose sight of why we're doing it.

So here's my proposal.

When NutriSnap reaches version 1.0, we'll look back at this conversation.

Not because of the code.

But because this is the moment the mission was born.
